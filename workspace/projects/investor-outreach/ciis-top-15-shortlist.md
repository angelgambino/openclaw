# CIIS Ketamine Clinic — Top 15 Donor Shortlist

## 1. Steve Cohen / Cohen Foundation (Score: 4.30)
He literally builds mental health clinics — 30+ nationwide through Cohen Veterans Network, $275M+ committed. This isn't a stretch; it's his exact model with a training component added. Go through CVN program staff. Ask: $1M-$2M. The "you've built 30, let's build the next one" pitch writes itself.

## 2. Thomas Insel / Former NIMH Director (Score: 4.15)
The most important name on this list — not for his checkbook ($50K-$100K) but for his endorsement. 13 years running NIMH makes him THE authority on mental health innovation. He's Bay Area-based. If he says "this clinic meets the standard," every other donor on this list pays attention. Approach directly through CIIS/Bay Area mental health networks. Secure him as an advisor first, donor second.

## 3. Tim Ferriss (Score: 4.10)
Has donated $2M+ specifically to psychedelic research (Johns Hopkins, MAPS, Imperial College). Nobody in the individual donor world has funded this space more visibly. The pitch: "You funded the research. This funds the delivery." Hard to reach cold — need a strong founder-network intro. Ask: $500K-$1M.

## 4. Rick Doblin / MAPS (Score: 4.10)
40+ years building the psychedelic therapy field. MAPS already trains therapists — CIIS clinic is a natural expansion of the training pipeline he's spent his career building. Existing CIIS/MAPS relationships should provide a warm path. Ask: $250K-$500K. The training angle is your strongest hook.

## 5. Ekaterina Malievskaia / Usona Institute (Score: 4.05)

The angle: "Ketamine is the legal bridge to what you're building with psilocybin — it's deployable NOW." Risk: she may see ketamine as not frontier enough. Ask: $500K-$1M.

## 6. Jack Kornfield / Spirit Rock (Score: 4.05)
Not a big-check donor — but Spirit Rock (Marin County) and CIIS share DNA in the contemplative/consciousness community. His endorsement carries enormous weight in the Bay Area contemplative world. Easy to reach through CIIS faculty. Start here for community validation, then leverage his network for bigger donors.
