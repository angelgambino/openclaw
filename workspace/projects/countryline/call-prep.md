# CountryLine — Monday Call Prep for Angel

## 1. DECK FEEDBACK (Honest Assessment)

### What's Strong
- **Clear thesis:** "No one owns the fan relationship for country music outside the US" — this is a real gap
- **C2C case study is powerful:** 100K attendees, embedded not peripheral, physical + digital activation
- **Multi-revenue model:** Not dependent on a single stream (membership, brand, events, commerce, data)
- **Timing narrative:** Country = fastest-growing global genre. True — Morgan Wallen, Beyoncé's Cowboy Carter, Lainey Wilson crossover all validate this
- **$1M ARR in the UK** is meaningful traction for a pre-seed/seed stage company in this space

### What Needs Work
- **"Superbrand" is dangerous framing** — investors hear that and think "ego project." Reframe as "platform" or "infrastructure" 
- **No unit economics shown** — what's the LTV? CAC? Churn on membership? This will be the first question from any serious investor
- **US expansion strategy is vague** — saying "scale globally" isn't a plan. Nashville is the obvious beachhead but how?
- **Team slide is missing** from the 1-pager — Simon's background isn't shown. Investors bet on people first
- **Competitive landscape missing** — what about Spotify's country playlists? CMT? Grand Ole Opry's digital presence? Apple Music Nashville? They need a "why not them" answer
- **Data/SaaS layer is hand-wavy** — "emerging" = "we haven't built it yet." Either show it or don't mention it
- **No clear funding ask** — how much are they raising? What stage? What's the use of funds?

### Verdict
This is a **seed-stage fan platform business** with real traction in one market (UK) and a credible thesis. It's NOT a media company — the deck should never say "media." It should say **"fan infrastructure"** or **"audience platform."** The best comp is something like **Fandom (valued at $4B)** for country music, not another music blog.

## 2. FUNDRAISING STRATEGY RECOMMENDATION

### Stage & Size
- **Round:** Seed (or large pre-seed)
- **Target:** $1.5M–$3M
- **Valuation range:** $8M–$15M pre-money (depending on ARR quality and retention metrics)
- **Instrument:** Priced round preferred given $1M ARR. SAFE only if moving fast

### Investor Profile (Best Fit)
1. **Music-tech / entertainment VCs** — they get the thesis immediately (e.g., Raised in Space, First Floor, Electric Feel Ventures)
2. **Creator economy / community VCs** — they understand fan platforms (e.g., Atelier Ventures, Night Ventures, Slow Ventures)
3. **UK-based consumer/media funds** — for the UK market credibility (e.g., Fuel Ventures, JamJar, Passion Capital)
4. **Strategic angels from music industry** — Nashville artists, managers, label execs who'd open doors in the US
5. **Brand/commerce VCs** — if the commerce layer is real (e.g., Forerunner, Coefficient Capital)

### Positioning by Investor Type
| Investor Type | Pitch Angle |
|---|---|
| Music-tech VC | "We own the fan relationship for the fastest-growing genre globally. No one else has this position." |
| Creator/Community VC | "This is Fandom meets Patreon for country music — owned audience, subscription, commerce, events." |
| Consumer/Media VC | "Country fans are the most loyal, highest-spending music demographic. We're the platform they don't have yet." |
| Strategic Angel (Nashville) | "We're your distribution to the international audience you can't reach. Let's build this together." |

### Fundraising Sequence
1. **Close UK/European music-tech investors first** — they understand the C2C traction
2. **Use that momentum for US music-tech VCs** — Raised in Space, Electric Feel, etc.
3. **Add Nashville strategic angels** — artists, managers, label execs (social proof + US doors)
4. **Top off with consumer/community VCs** — for the platform narrative

### Red Flags to Pressure-Test
- Is the $1M ARR recurring or is it events/sponsorship revenue that resets each year?
- What's the membership retention? (If <70% annual retention, this is an events business, not a platform)
- How much of revenue is UK-only vs. international?
- Does Simon have a US co-founder/hire lined up? Without US leadership, US expansion is aspirational
- What's the IP moat? If a label or Spotify builds a country community tomorrow, what protects CountryLine?

## 3. QUESTIONS TO ASK SIMON (Monday Call)

### Understanding the Business
1. **"Walk me through the $1M ARR — what's the breakdown by revenue stream?"** (membership vs brand vs events vs commerce)
2. **"What's the membership retention rate? Monthly and annual?"**
3. **"How many paying members vs. free registered users?"**
4. **"What's the average revenue per user (ARPU)?"**
5. **"What does the P&L look like? Are you profitable in the UK?"**

### Understanding the Opportunity
6. **"What's your US strategy? Do you have anyone on the ground in Nashville?"**
7. **"Who are your top 3 artist relationships and would they publicly endorse/invest?"**
8. **"What's the competitive landscape? How do you think about Spotify, CMT, Apple Music Nashville?"**
9. **"If a major label decided to build a country fan platform, what's your moat?"**
10. **"What's the data/SaaS play you mentioned? How far along is that?"**

### Understanding the Raise
11. **"How much are you raising and at what valuation?"**
12. **"How much runway do you have?"**
13. **"Who's already committed or in DD?"**
14. **"What's the use of funds? How does this money specifically get you to Series A?"**
15. **"What does Series A look like? What metrics do you need to hit?"**

### Understanding Fit (For Your Decision)
16. **"What role are you looking for me to play — advisor, fractional CGO, or something bigger?"**
17. **"What's the equity/comp structure you're thinking about?"**
18. **"What decisions are you wrestling with right now that you need help on?"**
19. **"If I came on as CGO, what would success look like in 6 months?"**
20. **"Who else is on the team? What are the gaps?"**

### The CEO Question (If Relevant)
21. **"Are you open to bringing in a CEO, or are you committed to the CEO role yourself?"**
22. **"What's your biggest strength and what do you need someone else to do?"**
23. **"How do you and your co-founder divide responsibilities?"**

## 4. SHOULD YOU WORK WITH THEM? (Decision Framework)

### Take the role IF:
✅ $1M ARR is real recurring revenue (not one-time events)
✅ Membership retention is >70%
✅ Simon is coachable and welcomes strong partners (not a "my way" founder)
✅ Equity comp is meaningful (2-5% for fractional CGO, 5-15% for CEO)
✅ They have or will raise enough runway for 18+ months
✅ The US expansion plan is realistic, not just wishful
✅ You genuinely believe country music fandom is a venture-scale opportunity

### Walk away IF:
❌ Revenue is mostly events/sponsorship that resets annually
❌ Simon wants help but won't give real equity
❌ No clear path to US market (you'd be selling a UK-only story)
❌ Team is too thin and no plan to hire
❌ Valuation expectations are unrealistic given stage
❌ Simon doesn't want a real partner — just wants to use your name/network

### The Ideal Outcome
You come on as **fractional CGO** (not CEO — don't take the helm of someone else's vision unless they're explicitly stepping aside). You help them:
1. Nail the fundraising narrative
2. Build the investor pipeline (using your network)
3. Design the US expansion GTM
4. Bring strategic Nashville relationships

Comp: **$10-15K/month + 1-3% equity**, with a path to a larger role if the company hits milestones.

## 5. WHAT TO SEND THEM BEFORE THE CALL

Send the investor research sheet with a note like:

> "Simon — looking forward to Monday. I took a deep look at CountryLine's positioning and put together an initial investor target list of 25 high-fit investors across music-tech, creator economy, and media. I also have some thoughts on fundraising strategy and positioning. Let's use the call to align on the approach."

This does three things:

2. Demonstrates your value immediately (they see the investor list)
3. Sets the call agenda as strategic, not exploratory (you're already in action mode)
