# CountryLine — Top 10 Investor Shortlist

## 1. Jukebox Ventures (Priority Tier 1)
Jukebox is the single highest-priority investor for CountryLine. Launched in October 2025 as a UK-based, $15M fund focused exclusively on music-tech startups from pre-seed to Series A, their stated thesis covers fan engagement, content monetization, live experiences, and creator tools — which is essentially a description of CountryLine's business. They are actively deploying Fund I and actively seeking deal flow. **Approach angle:** Lead with the UK traction (~$1M ARR), the C2C Festival case study as proof of live experience integration, and the "platform layer for global country music" positioning that makes this a category-defining investment in an underserved genre vertical.

## 2. Mercuri (Priority Tier 1)
Mercuri is the premier UK early-stage fund at the intersection of media, entertainment, and technology — CountryLine's exact category. Founded by Alan Hudson with a £50M Fund II (backed by the Scott Trust/Guardian and British Business Bank), they lead pre-seed and seed rounds and take board seats. Their London Kings Cross base and media heritage make them culturally aligned with a UK-first media platform. **Approach angle:** Emphasize the media-tech infrastructure angle — CountryLine isn't just content, it's a platform with data/SaaS ambitions. The Guardian/Scott Trust connection also signals potential media partnership upside.

## 3. Crush Ventures (Priority Tier 1)
Crush Ventures was literally created to invest at the intersection of media, culture, and commerce, founded by the team behind Crush Music (a major artist management firm). Their thesis statement — "we invest in founders building the future of how talent will discover, engage, and monetize fans" — could have been written about CountryLine. Portfolio includes Beacons, Splice, and Create O/S. **Approach angle:** Lead with the artist relationship side — how CountryLine creates artist-fan connection loops, embedded content at C2C, and artist interview programming. The music management background means they'll immediately grasp the artist activation model.

## 4. Slow Ventures (Priority Tier 1)
Slow Ventures launched a $60M dedicated creator fund in February 2025, specifically backing creator-led businesses with niche community focus. Sam Lessin and team have a strong thesis on niche community monetization — exactly what CountryLine represents for country music globally. Their sweet spot ($500K-$2M checks) fits a seed round. **Approach angle:** Position CountryLine as the quintessential niche community business: a passionate, underserved global audience (country fans outside the US) with proven willingness to pay, creating a community-commerce flywheel.

## 5. Seven Seven Six (Priority Tier 1)
Alexis Ohanian's fund focuses on seed-stage creator economy startups, and as the co-founder of Reddit, he deeply understands community platforms and how passionate niche communities create outsized value. His fund has backed Pearpop, Dispo, and other community-first businesses. **Approach angle:** The Reddit playbook — a passionate niche community that becomes a global destination. Country music fans are one of the most engaged, loyal fan communities in music. Ohanian's personal accessibility via social media makes warm outreach easier than most VCs.

## 6. Imaginary Ventures (Priority Tier 2)
Imaginary's 2024 seed investment in Hook Music (a music fan platform for social media remixes) proves they have specific appetite for music + fan + social intersection companies. Founded by Natalie Massenet (Net-a-Porter) and Nick Brown, they understand consumer/community brands with commerce DNA. **Approach angle:** Explicitly reference the Hook Music investment as proof of thesis alignment, then differentiate: CountryLine has revenue, established audience, and a genre-specific moat that Hook doesn't.

## 7. Troy Carter (Priority Tier 2)
Troy Carter is perhaps the most connected music-tech angel investor in the world. Former manager of Lady Gaga, early Spotify advisor, and active investor in music-tech startups, he brings extraordinary signal value and strategic introductions to both the US music industry and the broader tech ecosystem. **Approach angle:** The "country music is the fastest-growing global genre" narrative combined with CountryLine's UK-first success story. Carter understands artist-fan dynamics and international music market expansion intimately.

## 8. Night Ventures (Priority Tier 2)
Night Ventures' unique LP base of 50+ top creators (MrBeast, etc.) gives them unparalleled understanding of fan engagement, audience monetization, and creator tools. They've invested in Moonpay, Pearpop, and Beacons. **Approach angle:** Lead with the audience metrics — hundreds of thousands of followers, engaged member base, and the data/monetization layer. Night Ventures will understand the fan platform model intuitively and can provide strategic distribution value through their creator network.

## 9. UTA Ventures (Priority Tier 2)
As the investment arm of United Talent Agency, UTA Ventures offers strategic capital with talent network access. They represent country artists and can create direct artist partnership pipelines. **Approach angle:** The dual value proposition — capital plus artist access. Position CountryLine as a distribution and fan activation channel that benefits UTA's own artist roster, making it a strategic investment rather than purely financial.

## 10. SignalFire (Priority Tier 2)
SignalFire's data-driven approach and deep creator economy thesis (they published the leading Creator Economy Market Map) make them a sophisticated partner for CountryLine's data/SaaS ambitions. They invest seed to Series B with $1M-$5M checks. **Approach angle:** Lead with the data layer — CountryLine's audience data, behavioral insights, and emerging SaaS capabilities. SignalFire's quantitative approach means they'll respond to metrics on audience engagement, retention, and monetization efficiency.
